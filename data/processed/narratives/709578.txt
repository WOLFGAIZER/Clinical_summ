Here is a concise summary of the WTTS events:

A 72-year-old woman presented to the Emergency Department with onset of oppressive chest pain associated with dyspnea and lipothymia. The patient had a history of cardiovascular risk factors, including arterial hypertension, overweight, and known chronic hyponatremia. She also had hypothyroidism on replacement therapy, unclear diagnosis of COPD or asthma, and family history of Brugada syndrome.

The patient was previously treated for paroxysmal atrial fibrillation with electrical cardioversion in 2019 and coronary angiograms in 2007, 2019, and 2020. She had a reported left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) of 52% in 2019 and 45% in 2020 with apical and mid-segment hypokinesia.

Upon presentation, the patient's symptoms persisted despite administration of nitispray, and serial ECGs showed increased ST depression. A chest X-ray was normal, and blood chemistry tests were negative for troponin (TnT) in two consecutive tests. The patient remained asymptomatic with a normal physical examination. An echocardiogram revealed hypertrophic left ventricle with LVEF of about 50% and mild mitral regurgitation.

The clinical assessment concluded that the patient's chest pain and ECG abnormalities were not accompanied by enzymatic activity in the blood, warranting further evaluation via coronary CT.
