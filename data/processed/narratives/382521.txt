A 66-year-old male presented to the Emergency Department with a reported volumetric increase of his left hemiscrotum, which was not painful. He has a history of radical cystoprostatectomy and orthotopic bladder substitution with Camey II procedure in 2016. On physical examination, bilateral hydrocele and a non-painful taut-elastic swelling were noted on the left side. An abdominal CT scan revealed a cystic neoplasm starting from the funiculus, involving the entire inguinal canal up to the abdomen. The clinical situation was discussed with the patient, and exploratory surgery for removal of the mass and possible left orchifunicolectomy was recommended.
