On January 1, 2026, an 85-year-old male patient with DMT2, IRC, K prostate with bone MTS was admitted to ER due to asthenia and deterioration of general conditions. He reported chest pain at rest on the previous Thursday, which resolved after taking paracetamol. In ER, his blood pressure was 115/88 mmHg, heart rate was 90/min, and electrocardiogram showed ST-segment elevation in V1-V3. An echocardiogram revealed left ventricular hypertrophy, EF of 55%, a mid-apical interventricular septal defect (11 mm breach) with a left-right shunt. A mild myocardial infarction was also detected.

Subsequent evaluations showed signs of right ventricular volume overload, dilated atria, and normal pericardium. The patient's blood test results were within normal limits except for elevated troponin levels. It was decided to proceed with diagnostic coronary angiography and mechanical support with intra-aortic balloon pumping (IABP).
