Here is the clinical summary:

On January 1, 2026, a 77-year-old patient presented to the emergency department with sudden onset of generalized tonic-clonic (TC) seizures in all four limbs, preceded by screams. The patient has a past medical history of cognitive impairment, hearing loss, arterial hypertension, previous Guillain-Barré syndrome, spinal stabilization surgery, and various other conditions. A brain CT scan showed diffuse cortico-subcortical atrophy with no acute manifestations. Neurological examination revealed the patient to be alert and cooperative, but partially oriented in time and place. The patient had bilateral plantar reflexes in flexion. The diagnosis was made of generalized TC seizures due to moderate cerebral atrophy and cognitive impairment.
