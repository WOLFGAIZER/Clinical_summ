A patient with advanced chronic renal insufficiency (CRI) and history of peritoneal dialysis, was admitted to the Emergency Department on January 1, 2026, for pain and hypoesthesia at the injection site in the left arm. The pain subsided after removing the intravenous access for dialysis. The patient had a past medical history of various conditions, including dyslipidemia, arterial hypertension, erosive antral gastropathy, BPH, lower limb vasculopathy, and previous interventions such as PTA/stenting and coronary bypass surgery. On physical examination, the patient was alert, oriented, and cooperative with no pain, sensory-motor deficits, or lesions. The AVF functioned properly with direct flows on IO and radial arteries. No indications for vascular surgery were noted, and it was recommended to vary the injection site for future dialysis sessions.
