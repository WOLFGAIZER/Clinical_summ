Here is the clinical summary:

A patient presenting with dyslipidemia, arterial hypertension, and benign prostatic hyperplasia was brought to the emergency room for a focal seizure with bilateral tonic-clonic evolution. The patient had no prior history of neurological disorders. Initial examination revealed normal neurological findings, including equal pupils and intact cranial nerves. However, the patient reported recent episodes of fleeting subjective feeling of confusion. A brain CT scan showed leukoaraiosis and right frontal ischemic outcome. The patient was started on lacosamide therapy with close monitoring for potential side effects. Follow-up appointments were scheduled to monitor blood count, sodium, potassium, creatinine, and liver enzymes, as well as electrocardiogram and PR interval.
