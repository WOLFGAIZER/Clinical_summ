Here is the clinical summary:

On January 1, 2026, an 81-year-old patient was admitted to a nursing home after being found with right gaze deviation and left hemiplegia. The patient had a past medical history of DM2 with polyneuropathy, AF not on OAT, CRI due to single kidney (surgically removed), and unoperated left femur fracture. A brain CT was performed, showing evidence of subacute ischemia in the right lenticular capsule, likely hyperdensity in the M1-M2 passage of the right MCA. Neurological examination revealed alertness, head and eyes deviated to the right, mute, left facial nerve deficit of the inferior branch, probable homonymous hemianopsia on the left side, left limb plegia, and possible dysphagia. The patient was treated with enoxaparin 2000 U/day for CRI and recommended IV hydration, fasting for probable dysphagia, follow-up brain CT, and consideration of hospitalization in a medical setting given the lack of further neurological indications and multiple comorbidities.
